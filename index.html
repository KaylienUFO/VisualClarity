What is Visual Snow Syndrome and how do games effect it?

Visual Snow Syndrome (VSS) is a neurological perceptual condition where individuals experience persistent visual static, light sensitivity, and visual distortion effects.

Mainstream games often assume a "default visual experience" that not all players share, priortizing cinematic desity over readabiltiy, leading to unintentionally exculding users with visual conditions. This artefact explores how game HUD and graphic systems can be adapted to reduce visual overload, improve readability, and introduce game accessibility; specifically for VSS players.

How do these settings support VSS-affected users?

To understand the necessity of these settings, its important to recognize the visual differences present in
VSS before attempting to provide a visual fix. Below are observed side effects of VSS with designed solutions
catered to the specifc issue; which is only then adapted to a customizable setting.


Problem Design Response Setting
Low contrast between HUD elements and environments can make objects/elements difficult to distinguish, especially during a visual overload. Allow players to increase contrast between interface elements and the game world to increase readability and visual separation. UI Contrast
Small text can become difficult to read when vision is blurred or distorted, causing strained eyes and irritation. Enable players to enlarge the interface and menu text for improved readability and reduced eye strain. Font Size
Important HUD information may become visually lost during intense effects or cluttered scenes due to an inability to focus clearly. Let players increase HUD size so critical gameplay elements remain visible and understandable. HUD Scale
General environments and screens can cause excessive brightness to VSS users, as VSS is often accompanied by Photophobia (light sensitivity) this leads to visual discomfort and fatigue. Reduce overall screen brightness to create a softer and less visually aggressive experience. Screen Dim
With Photophobia, strong bloom effects can obscure details, and can overwhelm the scene. Give players the option to disable bloom effects for clearer image readability. Bloom Effect Toggle
Motion blur can reduce visual clarity during movement, and can heighten nausea and motion sickness; specifically for VSS users affected heavily by Palinopsia (persistent afterimages). Allow motion blur to be disabled to maintain sharper visuals, visual stability, and reduce double blur for VSS players. Motion Blur Toggle
Constant static on the eye, paired with looking at crisp solid coloured environments, can cause eye irritation and headaches if the static is focused on for too long. Surprisingly, some people with VSS have found using static overlay plugins on devices can actually reduce real VSS static, with the artificial overlay almost freezing their vision’s static. Letting players have the option to turn on an adjustable static overlay provides visual relief to users. Static Overlay
The visual exposure that VSS users have is increased by Photophobia, but also by a blue field entoptic phenomenon (tiny white flickering dots). These visual disturbances are typically caused by colour intensity and glare. On the otherside, when the screen is too dark it can appear illegiable due to VSS Nyctalopia (night blindness). Allowing players to reduce or increase the exposure to their specific needs improves personal visual comfort per user. Exposure
A per user problem is colour saturation, although not affecting all, some VSS users can have colours appear more or less saturated due to their visual static in combination with other symptoms. The option to increase or reduce screen saturation can help certain users emulate how they believe the game is meant to be visually represented, adjusted for their vision. Saturation
Screen shake effects can cause motion discomfort and nausea. Due to the brain’s struggle to process visual input properly, camera shake creates an even heightened sense of motion. Allow players to disable screen shake for a more stable and comfortable gaming experience, without having to skip interaction cutscenes. Screen Shake Toggle
Simulated camera sway movements can contribute to dizziness and disorientation, causing symptoms like afterimages and headaches to increase. Making the camera sway toggable reduces movement intensity and reduces motion-related discomfort. Camera Motion Toggle
Flashing effects in games can greatly cause visual irritation because of the heighted likelihood of an afterimage remaining for seconds after, due to the brightness and unexpectedness of the flash. Allowing flashing effects to be disabled creates a safer experience and visual clarity for VSS users. Flashing Effects Toggle
